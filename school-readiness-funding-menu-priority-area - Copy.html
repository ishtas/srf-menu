School Readiness Funding (SRF) menu by priority area

SRF menu priority areas are categorised as communication, wellbeing and access and inclusion.

On this page:

  • Communication (language development)
  • Wellbeing (social and emotional)
  • Access and Inclusion

Communication (language development)

Abecedarian Approach Australia (3a)

The Abecedarian Approach Australia (3a) uses specific teaching and learning strategies and 'LearningGames' in early childhood environments and parenting programs.

Additional Educators

'Additional Educators' can be engaged by services with significant levels of educational disadvantage and children with vulnerability characteristics.

Animal Fun*

Animal Fun is a play-based program designed to improve the health and wellbeing of young children.

Wellbeing (social and emotional)

A SECRET

ASECRET is a framework to help childrens social and emotional wellbeing,including their self-regulation.

ACF Bringing Up Great Kids

This practical one-day workshop provides participants with theskills to successfully establish and run the 'Bringing Up Great Kids' parenting program.

Be You

Be You offers a Framework, professional learning resources,tools and consultant supportto guide services in supporting the mental health and wellbeing of educators, children and their families.

Access and Inclusion

Koorie Heritage Trust

A range of programs and servicessuch asa cultural education servicethat includesguided walking tours, cultural competency training and programs developed specifically for schools and educators.

Father Inclusive Practice Guide

Positive and consistent fatherchild interactions give children an increased sense of wellbeing, a clearer sense of identity and greater resilience.
